The CTO's Secret Weapon

Fortune 100 Experience

I’ve survived most of the ways a software project can fail. Through four decades in tech, over 50 projects, from startups to majors like Intel, Microsoft, Exxon, Boeing and NASDAQ, I know what fixes the faults and what doesn’t.

Proprietary Protocols

I’m not following someone else’s recipes and hoping they work. I created these six protocols for project repair and product delivery: I and my teams smoothly Orient, React, Assess, Probe, Select, and Act.

Fad-Free Guarantee

I don’t chase fads in computing or project management. Only code and practices that deliver tangible value should exist. A lot of errors and waste can be cut out; I hunt down fad-code and delete it, leaving only the productive essentials.

Plain English Executive Briefs

I clearly state only points that matter, whether it’s part of the problem, part of the failed solutions, part of my plan, or my implementation. No more technobabble snow jobs and excuses; know what you're paying for and know what you're getting.

Subject Matter Expertise

Having done software for companies ranging from Intel to NASDAQ and DoE, I have a truly rare mix of extreme hands-on technical skill married with the ability to create and lead effective software tiger teams.

Problem Solved, Solution Delivered

Good code makes working systems but it must be properly deployed. In the end the system’s components must be in place and operating properly in their real production environment. It's not a solution until it's rolled out and running smoothly the third time.

ROI guaranteed.
